This site has been moved

It’s that time of year when my yearly hosting plan requires renewal, and this year I’ve decided not to renew.  I’ve never been very happy with the speed of my shared hosting provider, but that’s not why I made the move.

The main reason for the move was SSL certificates.  Although I don’t think it is necessary for my personal blog to be sent to readers over a secure connection, the internet seems to think so.  The hosting provider I had before the move only allows one certificate issuer, and the prices are quite high.  Furthermore, I have never made any money off of this site, and it isn’t the type of site that would make much money.  Thus, I would like to use a free service such as Let’s Encrypt for my certificates.

I reached this realization about six months ago, but couldn’t take any action on it because I had already paid for a year of hosting.  At the time I planned to move to a more expensive service.  However, after further consideration, I decided that the more expensive hosting wasn’t worth that expense, and thus I should move to something free.

Several days of trial and error informed the migration progress which I am about to perform.  My goal is to keep all the links to blog posts the same.  I already want to keep my domain name for my email address, so this should work.  As my previous host goes offline, I suspect the text will remain available, but the images might not.  Thus I will be spending a few days repairing those links.

The current plan is to use a plugin called Simply Static to download all of my posts as static pages so they can live on at their original addresses.  As a result, some features weren’t going to work properly.  For example, I had to disable search and commenting.

For those who visit my site regularly, thank you for your interest in what I do.  Although my vision for my site may change over time, I still want to keep something interesting in my corner of the web.

-Neil
